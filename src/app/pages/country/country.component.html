Overview of Belarus ​

Around 9.5 million people live in Belarus. The official languages are Belarusian and Russian. Other languages spoken are Polish, Ukrainian and Yiddish. Minsk is the capital and the largest city in Belarus. The other major cities are Gomel, Mogilev, Vitebsk, Grodno, Brest, Babruysk,Baranavichy, Barysaw, Pinsk.

Belarus occupied 53rd position among 189 countries in UN’s human development index (HDI) in 2018.The living, dwelling and educational cost are lower in Belarus than in majority of western and eastern European countries.

Belarus’s GDP (PPP) is $201 billion and the nominal GDP is 60 billion in 2018. The sector wise contribution to GDP is services (51%), industry (41%) and agriculture (8%). Belarusian rouble is the official currency of Belarus (Symbol: Br). The rouble is further subdivided into 100 kapeks. 1 Belarusian Rouble equals0.41 Euro.

90% of the population in Belarus have higher secondary or basic education making it one of the countries with most educated, highly qualified and industrious people. Strategic investors can buy a businessin Belarus or invest in a business in Belarus.

The major export goods are machinery and equipment, mineral products, chemicals, metals, textiles, foodstuffs. The main export partners are Russia (46%), Ukraine (12%), UK (5%), Germany (4%). The major import goods are mineral products, machinery and equipment, chemicals, foodstuffs, metals. The main import partners are Russia (55%), China (8%), Germany (5%), Poland (4.5%).

Annually over 100 million tonnes of European cargo cross Belarus’s territory. 90% of the cargo is between Russia and the EU. In 2018, considerable investment volumes were channelled into companies in Russia (68.2% of the total), Ukraine (16.5%), and the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland (6.7%).
