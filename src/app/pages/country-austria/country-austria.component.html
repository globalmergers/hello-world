Overview of Austria ​

Austria is a federal republic state with a parliamentary democracy, consisting of 9 independent federal regions (Burgenland, Carinthia, Lower Austria, Upper Austria, Salzburg, Styria, Tirol, Vorarlberg, and Vienna) with their own provincial governments. Austria has an area of 83.858 sq. km with 8.8 million inhabitants.

Austria is bordered by the Czech Republic and Germany to the north, Hungary, and Slovakia to the east, Slovenia and Italy to the south, and Switzerland and Liechtenstein to the west. Austria is the 12th richest country in the world in terms of GDP per capita, has a well-developed social market economy, and a high standard of living.

The major political parties of Austria are Austrian People's Party, Social Democratic Party of Austria, Freedom Party of Austria, NEOS – The New Austria and Liberal Forum, NOW – List Pilz, The Greens – The Green Alternative. The major cities in Austria are Vienna (capital), Graz, Linz, Salzburg, Innsbruck, Klagenfurt, Villach, Wels, Sankt Polten, Dornbirn.

The main languages spoken in Austria are German (official), Alemannic, Austro-Bavarian. Minority languages spoken are Turkish, Serbian, Burgenland Croatian, Hungarian and Slovene. The schilling was the currency of Austria from 1925 to 1938 and from 1945 to 1999, and the circulating currency until 2002. Now the currency used in Austria is Euro (€).
